What the product does

A decision gate—not another confidence score.

Use Chiron when a system needs to distinguish a claim it can support from one it cannot. It is built for exact, inspectable checks and explicit refusal—not plausible-looking output.

01 / INPUT

Send the claim or structured surface.

Try a machine-generated mathematical or factual claim in the live checker, or give the engine a sequence whose rule needs to be recovered.

02 / VERDICT

Get VERIFIED, REFUTED, or REFUSED.

Chiron checks only the portion it can handle exactly. It does not bless the surrounding prose just because one statement passed.

03 / EVIDENCE

Keep the reason, not just the answer.

Results include the discovered rule or claim-level status, a machine-readable evidence record, and the limits of coverage. Try it live →

The important constraint

Honest scope is a feature, not a footnote.

A system that says “verified” when it only recognized part of the input is worse than no system at all. Chiron surfaces coverage and leaves uncheckable material unblessed.

It can checkSupported exact claim types and structured patterns, with an explanation of the verdict.
It will refuseInputs outside its proof methods, ambiguous evidence, or work beyond the configured budget.
It does not claimTo certify all free-form language, business judgment, legal compliance, or safety in the abstract.

Who gets value now

For teams that need evidence before an output becomes an action.

A strong fit

  • AI product engineers who need a gate around supported claims.
  • Evaluation owners who need reproducible pass, fail, and abstain records.
  • Teams that want a deterministic engine they can run offline under a commercial license.

Not the promise

  • A generic hallucination score or a replacement for domain experts.
  • A legal, regulatory, or security conclusion about arbitrary text.
  • A black-box “trust us” layer that silently turns uncertainty into approval.
